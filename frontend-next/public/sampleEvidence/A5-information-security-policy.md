## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all information assets owned, controlled, or processed by **[Organization Name]**. It applies to all employees, contractors, third-party service providers, and any individual with access to organizational information systems.

## 2. Scope

This policy covers:
- All information assets (digital, physical, and intellectual)
- All information processing facilities and systems
- All personnel with access to organizational information
- All locations where organizational information is processed or stored

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Information Classification
All information shall be classified according to its sensitivity:
- **Restricted**: Board-level strategic data, encryption keys, authentication credentials
- **Confidential**: Customer PII, financial records, HR data, source code
- **Internal**: Internal communications, project documentation, procedures
- **Public**: Marketing materials, published reports, public website content

### 3.2 Access Control
- Access to information systems shall be granted based on the principle of **least privilege**
- All access requests must be approved by the data owner and IT Security
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all remote access and privileged accounts
- Access rights shall be reviewed quarterly

### 3.3 Acceptable Use
- Company information systems shall be used for authorized business purposes only
- Personal use is permitted within reasonable limits and must not compromise security
- Users must not attempt to bypass security controls or access unauthorized resources

### 3.4 Incident Management
- All security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center (SOC) within **1 hour** of detection
- The Incident Response Team (IRT) shall classify, contain, and remediate incidents per the Incident Response Plan (ISMS-IRP-001)
- Post-incident reviews shall be conducted within 5 business days

### 3.5 Business Continuity
- Critical systems must maintain **99.9% availability** (RPO: 1 hour, RTO: 4 hours)
- Disaster recovery tests shall be conducted semi-annually
- Business impact analysis shall be updated annually

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Board of Directors | Approve ISMS policy, allocate resources |
| CISO | Overall ISMS governance, risk management |
| IT Security Team | Implement controls, monitor compliance |
| Department Heads | Ensure staff compliance within their units |
| All Employees | Follow security policies, report incidents |

## 5. Compliance

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Violations may also be subject to legal action where applicable.

## 6. Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or when significant changes occur to the organization, technology, or threat landscape.
